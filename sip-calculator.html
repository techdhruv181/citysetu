What is a SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a disciplined way of investing in mutual funds. It allows investors to allocate a fixed amount of money at regular intervals (such as monthly or quarterly) into a mutual fund scheme. This approach helps in mitigating market volatility through rupee-cost averaging and builds significant wealth over the long term thanks to the power of compounding.

How does our SIP Calculator work?

Our free SIP calculator uses the standard compound interest formula to project the future value of your investments. The formula generally used is: FV = P × ({[1 + i]^n - 1} / i) × (1 + i)

  • FV is the Future Value of the investment.
  • P is the fixed Monthly Investment amount.
  • i is the expected monthly return rate (annual rate divided by 12).
  • n is the total number of months you will be investing.

Benefits of Investing via SIP

Investing through a Systematic Investment Plan offers several advantages. First, it instills financial discipline, requiring you to save a specific amount regularly. Second, it leverages rupee-cost averaging, meaning you buy more mutual fund units when the market is low and fewer when the market is high, ultimately lowering your average cost per unit. Lastly, the power of compounding ensures that even small investments made consistently over a 10 to 20-year period can grow into a massive financial corpus.
